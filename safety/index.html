Safety

Trust is built with checks, proof, and clear reporting.

Barrtar is in closed testing, so safety is being tuned with real feedback. The goal is simple: make local tasks easier without pretending every real-world risk disappears.

What is already part of the flow

ID verification for doers

Users can create tasks easily, but people accepting and working on tasks may need ID verification first.

Task review and limits

Tasks can go through checks, and creators have active-task limits to reduce spam and misuse.

Direct-payment proof

Payments happen directly between users. Proof can be uploaded when useful for support or disputes.

Reports and disputes

Users can report problems and raise task disputes inside the app for review.

Start OTP check

Creators should verify that the person who arrived matches the accepted doer before sharing the OTP that starts work.

Safety expectations for users

  • Only post lawful, safe, clearly scoped tasks.
  • Do not accept work that feels unsafe or outside your ability.
  • Agree scope, amount, timing, and proof before starting.
  • Creators should not share the start OTP until they are comfortable the matched person is the same person who arrived.
  • If the person does not match or something feels wrong, use release, deny, report, or support instead of starting the task.
  • Use in-app reports or support if something feels suspicious.
  • Do not share unnecessary sensitive information with other users.

Important limitation

Barrtar can add verification, checks, reports, evidence, and account action, but it cannot guarantee that every user, payment, task, or real-world interaction will be risk-free. Closed testing helps improve these controls before wider launch.
