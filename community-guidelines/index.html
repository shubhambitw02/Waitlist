Community guidelines

Keep Barrtar useful, legal, and safe for local work.

Barrtar is for everyday help between people nearby. These rules protect creators, doers, testers, and the wider community.

Last updated: June 2, 2026 Applies to tasks, profiles, proof, and messages

Allowed tasks

Use Barrtar for lawful local tasks where the scope, place, time, price, and proof can be explained clearly. Examples include queue help, shopping help, packing, event support, promotion, small practical help, or skill support.

Not allowed

Illegal, unsafe, exploitative, deceptive, or harmful work.
Weapons, drugs, alcohol, tobacco, or regulated substances.
Sexual services, explicit content, or exploitation.
Harassment, stalking, intimidation, hate, or threats.
Tasks involving minors or improper contact with minors.
Professional medical, legal, financial, or emergency services.
Dangerous work without lawful training, tools, or permission.
Fraud, identity misuse, fake reviews, scams, or academic cheating.

Respect and proof

Be clear about expectations before the task starts. Keep communication respectful. If you upload evidence, payment proof, or completion proof, submit only information relevant to the task or dispute.

Before work starts

Creators should check that the person who arrived matches the accepted doer shown in Barrtar before sharing the start OTP. If the person does not match, refuses reasonable identity checking, or something feels wrong, do not share the OTP. Use the app's release, deny, report, or support paths instead.

Reporting and disputes

Report unsafe tasks, suspicious users, payment issues, harassment, or policy violations in the app or at support@barrtar.com. Include task details and proof where safe to do so.

What Barrtar may do

Barrtar may remove tasks, limit visibility, pause matching, restrict accounts, request verification, preserve evidence, or cooperate with lawful requests when needed to protect people and platform integrity.
