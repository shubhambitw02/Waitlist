Terms

Clear rules for local peer-to-peer work.

Barrtar helps people post local tasks or earn by accepting nearby tasks. Users provide the task service to each other. Barrtar does not charge a platform fee and does not hold user funds in v1.

Last updated: June 2, 2026 Closed testing

1. Who can use Barrtar

Barrtar is intended for adults. You must use Barrtar only for lawful, safe, respectful tasks and provide accurate account, profile, task, and verification information when requested.

2. Peer-to-peer tasks

Task creators are responsible for describing the work clearly, choosing a location, setting the amount, and coordinating with the doer. Doers choose tasks they are comfortable doing and should only accept work they can complete safely and lawfully.

3. Direct payments

In Barrtar v1, payments happen directly between the creator and doer, such as by UPI or cash. Barrtar does not process platform payments, does not hold funds, and does not guarantee payment recovery. Users are responsible for agreeing the amount, proof, and any records or taxes that apply to them.

4. Verification and task access

Users can create tasks easily. Users who want to accept and work on tasks may need to complete ID verification before taking up work. Barrtar may also review tasks or accounts to reduce unsafe, illegal, spammy, or abusive use.

5. Start OTP and in-person verification

When a task requires a creator-shared start OTP, the creator should check that the person who arrived matches the accepted doer shown in Barrtar before sharing that OTP. If the person does not match or the situation feels unsafe, the creator should not share the OTP and should use the app's release, deny, report, or support paths.

6. Prohibited tasks and conduct

Do not post, accept, request, or complete tasks that are illegal, unsafe, exploitative, deceptive, or harmful. See the Community Guidelines for examples including regulated goods, sexual services, weapons, drugs, harassment, fraud, and professional medical, legal, financial, or emergency services.

7. Disputes and enforcement

If a problem happens, users can raise a dispute in the app. Barrtar may review task details, submitted proof, payment proof, dispute evidence, reports, and account history. Barrtar may remove tasks, restrict accounts, preserve evidence, or cooperate with lawful requests when required.

8. Changes and contact

Barrtar may update these Terms as the product, law, or safety requirements change. For questions, contact support@barrtar.com.
